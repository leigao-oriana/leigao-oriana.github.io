About Me

I am Lei, a fourth-year PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at University College London, under the supervision of Prof.Sriram Subramanian and Associate Prof.Diego Martinez Plasencia. I currently do research at Multi-Sensory Devices (MSD) Group and create novel applications in HCI using acoustic levitation technique, exploring the design principles, technique solutions and future research possibilities.

My Research

I am deeply engaged in the field of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), with a particular emphasis on the development of innovative interactive prototypes employing advanced technologies such as Acoustic Levitation. My expertise extends to a wide range of areas including Haptics, Virtual/Augmented/Extended Reality (VR/AR/XR) systems, and using data-driven methods in creating efficient and impactful applications.
